About the workshop

The workshop behind the tags

Artisan & Oak is three people, a couple of kilns, a lot of walnut sawdust, and a shared rule: if we wouldn't give it as a gift ourselves, it doesn't leave the shop.

Our story

From one cutting board to a full workshop

Artisan & Oak started in 2019 in a one-car garage. After a decade restoring commercial furniture, Maren Oak left to make a single wedding gift for a friend — a walnut cutting board with two names burned into the corner. That one board turned into a dozen more, then into a folding table at weekend markets, then into a lease on a narrow storefront with just enough room for a lathe and a kiln.

Talia and Jonah joined not long after, each bringing a craft the garage didn't have room for: wheel-thrown pottery and small-batch candle pouring. What hasn't changed is the shape of the work — someone tells us what they're picturing, and one of us makes it, start to finish, on our own bench.

We turn down orders when they'd mean rushing the work. We'd rather make forty pieces a month we're proud of than four hundred we're not.

— Maren, Talia & Jonah
